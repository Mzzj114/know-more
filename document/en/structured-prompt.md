## Structured Prompting

As practice deepens, people have gradually discovered that **structured prompts** are more stable and easier to reuse than scattered, casual descriptions.

A common structure can include the following parts:

- **Role / Context**: The role the model should play or the situation it is in
- **Task**: The specific goal to be accomplished
- **Input**: Relevant data or materials (if any)
- **Output Format**: How the results should be presented
- **Constraints**: Style, length, or other limitations
- **Examples (optional)**: Reference outputs
- **Initial Condition (Init)**: The actual task you want the model to complete given all the background information above
