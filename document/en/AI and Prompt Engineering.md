# AI and Prompt Engineering

## AI Known to Everyone

In recent years, generative artificial intelligence, represented by Large Language Models (LLMs), has developed rapidly and is widely used in scenarios such as writing, programming, and learning assistance. Users only need to input a segment of natural language, and the model can generate the corresponding text content.

However, in actual use, many people find that for the same problem, they sometimes get high-quality, clearly structured answers, while other times the answers appear vague or even off-topic. This difference often comes not from the model itself, but from the "Prompt" input to the model.

Prompts can be understood as the communication interface between humans and the model. They not only describe "what to do" but also imply "how to do it" and "in what context." Prompt engineering is precisely the method of constructing these inputs to stably obtain the expected results.

## Large Language Models and AI Applications

We should be careful to distinguish between Large Language Models(LLMs) and AI applications. A Large Language Model is a natural language processing model based on deep learning, consisting of matrices with hundreds of billions of parameters and various mathematical operations. The main function of this model is simply to predict the next token based on the previous context. The "AI" we are familiar with can do much more. They can act like assistants, have exquisite chat interfaces, remember what you've said, search for reliable information on the internet or in databases, and some can even run code or operate your devices. The first three functions existed when ChatGPT was first released, but they were actually peripheral functions gradually developed by people. Combining Large Language Models with these peripheral functions is what we commonly see as AI applications.

In PromptForGood, we will not only use prompt engineering to study how to optimize the output of large language models but also study tips for using AI applications.

> It is worth noting that some AI applications automatically use prompt engineering techniques. For example, the "thinking" of various chatbots is actually the program automatically using the Chain-of-Thought technique (or something similar) for the Large Language Model.

## Prompt Engineering

Google states that prompt engineering is "the art and science of designing and optimizing prompts."

The essence of prompt engineering is not to find the "most clever expression," but to express requirements more clearly, completely, and executably.

From practical experience:

- Vague inputs often lead to vague outputs
- Clear structures can significantly improve stability
- Prompts themselves can be designed, reused, and optimized

However, prompt engineering is not an isolated skill but a new way of learning and expressing. Mastering it means you can collaborate more effectively with artificial intelligence.
